**Thundergeddon** is a hybrid physical/digital game in which small robotic tanks battle each other on a real tabletop battlefield.

The installation is designed as a walk-up, spectator-friendly experience. Players join from their phones via a browser, take control of a tank, view a live first-person camera feed, and play short tactical matches based around movement, objective capture and positioning.

Matches would typically last around 10–15 minutes, with 2–10 active players depending on the mode. It should also work well for spectators, as the physical robots, battlefield and live gameplay are easy to watch and understand from the side.

The tanks themselves are small hand-made robots, approximately 13 cm × 8 cm, with:

- 3D-printed bodies
- custom ESP32-based electronics
- motorised tracks and turrets
- infrared shooting and directional hit detection
- first-person cameras
- RFID readers for capture points and shield recharge points

A Unity app controls the game logic, including turns, alliances, action points, shields and hit points. 

I would supervise the installation throughout, handle resets and charging, and run either structured matches or a more drop-in free-play/demo mode depending on demand.
